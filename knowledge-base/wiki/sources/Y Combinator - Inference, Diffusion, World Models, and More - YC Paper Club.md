# Y Combinator - Inference, Diffusion, World Models, and More - YC Paper Club

## Summary

This video records the first YC Paper Club session, a sequence of short research talks spanning speculative decoding, diffusion-based control, world models, deep-learning generalization, and pretraining under extreme compute. As a source, it is less a single thesis than a snapshot of what researchers and founders at the frontier considered strategically important in May 2026.

## Key claims

- Inference has become a first-class research and engineering surface, not just the deployment tail of training.
- Diffusion and video-model ideas are being pulled into robotics planning through model predictive control.
- World models are resurging as a central agenda for general-purpose policies, planning, and embodied intelligence.
- Generalization remains a core unresolved question behind scaling; better theory could improve controllability, not just explanation.
- Pretraining still appears to unlock new capabilities, which keeps the data-versus-compute frontier open rather than settled.

## Why it matters

This source broadens the vault from static articles and papers into live research-conversation artifacts. It connects several existing branches at once: inference efficiency, world modeling, and the LLM training pipeline. It also adds startup/operator context through Y Combinator's paper-club framing, which is useful because it shows which research directions felt actionable to founders as well as researchers.

## Tensions / open questions

- How much of the next capability jump will come from better inference algorithms versus larger training runs?
- Will world models become a general agent substrate, or stay strongest in robotics and control-heavy settings?
- Does understanding generalization mechanistically lead to new training methods, or mainly better retrospective explanation?
