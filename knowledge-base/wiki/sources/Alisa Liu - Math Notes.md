# Alisa Liu - Math Notes

## Summary

A focused probability / statistics / discrete-math reference assembled for a quantitative ("math") interview round — the author notes it was "all for a single fateful interview." It covers the standard distribution families, expectation/variance machinery, inequalities, conditioning, limit theorems, a set of reusable problem-solving patterns, a catalogue of classic probability puzzles, core statistics (MLE, bias–variance), and calculus refreshers. It is the math companion to [[Alisa Liu - The AI Research Job Search]] and fills the "math interview" cell of [[ML Research Interview Preparation]].

## Key claims / coverage

- **Distributions** — discrete (Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Geometric) and continuous (Uniform, Exponential, Gaussian), each with pmf/pdf, mean, variance; includes a description→concept cheat-map ("memoryless, waiting → geometric/exponential"; "trials with success & failure → Bernoulli/binomial").
- **Expectations & variance** — linearity of expectation, variance identities, law of total expectation/variance.
- **Inequalities** — Markov, Chebyshev, Jensen.
- **Conditional probabilities** — Bayes' theorem and conditioning.
- **Limit theorems** — law of large numbers, central limit theorem.
- **Problem types** — first-step (one-step) analysis, counting in expectation via indicators, max/min of n random variables, combinatorics, Markov chains.
- **Puzzles** — birthday problem, gambler's ruin, random walk, coupon collector, information-theoretic puzzles, reservoir sampling, secretary problem, Monty Hall, longest run of heads, balls into bins, path counting.
- **Statistics** — maximum likelihood estimation; bias–variance decomposition.
- **Math things** — Taylor series, derivative rules, handy equations.
