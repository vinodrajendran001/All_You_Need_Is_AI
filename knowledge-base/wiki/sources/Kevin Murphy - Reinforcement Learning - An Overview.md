# Kevin Murphy - Reinforcement Learning: An Overview

## Summary

This paper is a broad survey of reinforcement learning as a field of sequential decision making. It positions RL as a large umbrella that spans value-based, policy-based, model-based, multi-agent, offline, hierarchical, and intrinsically motivated methods, and it explicitly connects the survey to current work on training LLMs with RL.

## Key claims

- Reinforcement learning is best understood at the big-picture level as a family of methods for sequential decision making.
- A useful overview of modern RL needs to cover multiple methodological families rather than one algorithmic lineage.
- LLM training is now part of the RL landscape, not a separate concern outside it.
- A single survey source can serve as a high-level anchor before drilling into narrower subtopics.
