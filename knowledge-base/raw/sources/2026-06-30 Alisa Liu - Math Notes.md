## Section synopsis

- **Distributions.** The standard discrete and continuous families with their pmf/pdf, mean, and variance. The notes include a quick mapping from problem *description* to *concept*, e.g. "memoryless, waiting → geometric / exponential," "trials with success & failure → Bernoulli / binomial," "large sample, average → (normal via CLT)."
- **Expectations & variance.** Linearity of expectation, variance identities, law of total expectation/variance.
- **Inequalities.** Markov, Chebyshev, Jensen, and related bounds.
- **Conditional probabilities.** Bayes' theorem and conditioning techniques.
- **Limit theorems.** Law of large numbers and the central limit theorem.
- **Problem types.** Reusable solution patterns: first-step (one-step) analysis for Markov/recurrence problems, computing expectations by counting/indicator variables, distributions of max/min of n random variables, combinatorial counting, and Markov chains.
- **Puzzles.** A worked catalogue of classic probability interview puzzles (birthday, gambler's ruin, random walk, coupon collector, reservoir sampling, secretary problem, Monty Hall, longest run of heads, balls into bins, path counting) — the kind of "fun logic puzzles to serious derivations" a math interview draws from.
- **Statistics.** Maximum likelihood estimation and the bias–variance decomposition.
- **Math things.** Calculus refreshers used in derivations: Taylor series, derivative rules, and a set of handy equations.
