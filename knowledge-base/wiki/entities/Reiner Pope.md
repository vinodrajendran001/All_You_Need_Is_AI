# Reiner Pope

## What it is

AI hardware researcher and CEO of MatX, interviewed by Dwarkesh Patel about chip design, accelerator architecture, and the math of LLM training and serving.

## Why it matters here

Reiner Pope matters to this vault because his material connects low-level chip primitives to higher-level questions about LLM throughput, memory bandwidth, parallelism, and deployment economics.

## Notes

- The chip-design lecture works upward from logic gates to GPUs, TPUs, FPGAs, and the hardware/software tradeoffs behind them.
- The flashcards source broadens that hardware lens into cluster economics: batch size, MoE layout, pipeline parallelism, RL/pretraining/inference compute tradeoffs, and KV-cache-driven inference cost.
- He is described in the source as CEO of MatX and previously at Google working on software efficiency, compilers, and TPU architecture.
