# Eric Jang

## What it is

AI researcher and engineer interviewed by Dwarkesh Patel about rebuilding AlphaGo from scratch, reinforcement learning, and the prospects for automated AI research.

## Why it matters here

This source uses Eric Jang's AlphaGo reconstruction as a bridge between classical game AI, modern RL-for-LLMs questions, and agentic research workflows. He matters to this vault because he connects all three in one technically concrete discussion.

## Notes

- The interview describes him as most recently vice president of AI at 1X Technologies and previously a senior research scientist at Google DeepMind Robotics.
- His AlphaGo reconstruction highlights how search can provide dense supervision targets that are hard to reproduce with naive policy-gradient training.
- The same discussion also grounds a realistic picture of autoresearch loops: useful for experimentation and tuning, weaker at question selection and escaping dead ends.
